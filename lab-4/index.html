Addendum: Explanation of Stylistic Choices

I made several deliberate choices in styling my resume to enhance its readability and visual appeal. Here are the key decisions and their rationale:

Font Choices:

I opted for Georgia as the main font family with serif as fallback. This choice provides a traditional and elegant look, enhancing the professional appearance of the resume.

Sizes of Headings and Main Text Size:

For headings, I used <h1> for the main title "Resume - Bindu Madhuri Chalasani" to signify its importance as the highest-level heading. Subsequent headings like <h2> and <h3> were used hierarchically to maintain consistency and readability throughout the content. The main text size is set to 16px with a line height of 1.6 to ensure a comfortable reading experience.

Color Choices:

I chose dark (#333) text on a light background (#fff) to maintain contrast and readability. This combination ensures that text stands out clearly and is easy to read.

Margin and Padding Choices:

Throughout the document, I applied margins and paddings strategically to improve visual hierarchy and spacing. For example, a 20px margin on the body element provides space between content and screen edges, enhancing clarity and focus. Sections have a 40px bottom margin to visually separate content areas, while headers and footers have padding for additional spacing and aesthetic appeal.

HTML Abuse:

I corrected instances from previous assignments where HTML tags were chosen based on visual appearance rather than semantic meaning. For instance, ensuring the main title "Resume - Bindu Madhuri Chalasani" is marked up with <h1> to reflect its hierarchical importance.
